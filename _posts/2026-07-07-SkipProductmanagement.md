# what is PM?
- people who glue creative ideas to the engineers

## PM has different jobs at different stages of a startup

1. product market fit

Here, founders experiment rapidly to find what sticks with customers. 

2. post product market fit

Here, experimentation stops. Stop commuincation between teams and focus 100% on that product. You MUST meet customer expectations. 

3. hypergrowth

PM manages the organizations' scale, and has 2 goals:
- scale product to more users
- expand to adjacent product lines

4. late stage

PMS fight innovators dilemma, by creating zero to one products with large corporate structure

# the shift from info mover to product builder

Information movement is handled by AI agents (the forward deployed engineer is dying as a result).
- Middle managers will also die since they can't reinvent themselves.

the modern PM's value lies in judgement, decision-making, and having a strong opinion on what should be built and why.
- He argues this role is a lot more interesting

Merging roles (PMs, designers, and engineers)
- Modern product people are expected to be gritty and technical, often using AI tools to build and validate their ideas directly rather than just managing others who build them

- it becomes less about people management and more about system design. you need to decide what to build

# career advice
Your tech career is 40-50 years, averaging 15-18 jobs
YOUR GOAL: each job should build on the other. SO, ensure first role maximizes opportunities for the second

Now PMs are expected to be technical:
- You should leave a company to be part of one that grows faster than you
- You shouldn’t be the best in your company

## how to get a job as a college student?

Be modern
- Use latest tools (AI agents, claud code)
- Have opinion on what NEEDS to be BUILT
- IMPLEMENT stuff. Be hands on over learning theory

Learn beyond programming
- keep a systems mindset ( As knowledge becomes a service and AI handles more of the construction, the primary problem becomes: "Does it fit, should you build it, and how do you know if it's working?" )

This skill involves:
- Understanding how different stacks work and evolve
- Evaluating if a project aligns with a company's brand or system design
- Thinking about where a product fits into the broader technological landscape

Succeed in unstructured places
- thrive without structure and feedback (there are less managers)
- Maintain an Undying Urgency to ship and experiment

# random advice
Value customer needs over what is ‘most valuable’ to your perspective. For example nobody wanted 1 app to do all messaging (Google hangouts). Whatsapp purely focused on text messages.

It doesn’t matter how you start but how fast you improve it (speed of iteration)
